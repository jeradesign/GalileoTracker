Face Detector

What You’re Seeing:
This face detector draws a red circle around any faces it detects. (There’s a size threshold, so distant faces won’t be recognized.)

How It Works:
This face detector uses a technique called a cascade classifier to detect faces. The classifier scans across then entire image looking for haar-like features that match those of faces in its training database. It’s called a ‘cascade’ because each candidate region is run through a cascade of different detectors, any of which can reject it. A region is only marked as a facepasses if it passes all of the detectors.

What It’s Used For:
Face detection is widely used in from everything to digital cameras to security applications to digital photo albums.

Try and Notice:
Turn your head to either side, or tilt it slightly. Note that the face detector no longer recognizes your face. That’s because this face detector was only trained on full-face images. Other, more sophisticated face recognizers can recognize faces from a wider range of angles.
